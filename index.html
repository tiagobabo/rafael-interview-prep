Key Products — Know These!

Refrigerated Trailers (Reefers)

German: Kühlauflieger

Temperature-controlled trailers for transporting perishable goods (food, pharmaceuticals). Schmitz Cargobull's FERROPLAST technology is well-known. This is their flagship product line.

Curtain-sided Trailers (Tautliners)

German: Schiebeplanenauflieger

Flexible trailers with sliding curtain sides for easy side-loading. Popular for general cargo, palletised goods. Very common in European logistics.

Box Trailers (Dry Freight)

German: Kofferauflieger

Enclosed rigid-sided trailers for dry goods, furniture, electronics — anything that needs protection from weather but not temperature control.

Container Chassis

German: Containerchassis

Frames designed to carry shipping containers. Used in intermodal transport (ship-to-rail-to-road).

Tipper Trailers

German: Kipperauflieger

For bulk materials like grain, aggregates, and construction materials. Can tip to unload cargo.

Scenario-Based Questions

Scenario 1: Angry customer about delayed delivery

"A Romanian-speaking customer calls, very frustrated, saying their trailer was supposed to arrive last week and it hasn't. You don't have access to delivery tracking. What do you do?"

1. Stay calm, listen actively, acknowledge their frustration: "I completely understand your concern, and I'm sorry for the inconvenience."

2. Gather information: order number, name, delivery address, contact details.

3. Be honest: "I want to make sure you get accurate information, so I'm going to escalate this to our logistics team who can check the exact status."

4. Set expectations: "They will get back to you within [timeframe]. Can I confirm the best number to reach you on?"

5. Log everything in the system for follow-up.

Key: Never guess or make promises you can't keep. Empathy + accuracy + proper escalation.

Scenario 2: Customer asks technical questions you can't answer

"A customer calls asking about the specific temperature range of the S.KO COOL refrigerated trailer and whether it's compatible with their fleet's tracking system."

1. Thank them for the question and show interest.

2. Share what you know at a basic level: "Our refrigerated trailers are designed for a wide range of temperature-controlled transport."

3. For specifics: "For the exact technical specifications and compatibility, I'd like to connect you with our technical sales team who can give you the most accurate information."

4. Offer to arrange a callback or transfer the call.

Key: It's OK not to know everything — knowing when to escalate is a strength.
